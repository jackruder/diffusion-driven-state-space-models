* Overview / TL;DR

For an order-$j$ Markov SSM the encoder amortizes the *smoother conditional*
$q_\phi(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T}) = \mathcal N(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2)$,
where $\mathbf z_{<t}:=(z_{t-1},\dots,z_{t-j})$ is the $j$-lag /history window/.
The Gaussian is a /variational output form/, *not* an assumption about the SSM:
the generative transition is a diffusion and the emission a decoder, so the true
conditional can be arbitrary / nonparametric and non-Gaussian. (model-v2 presents
$j=1$; this doc keeps $j$ general.)

*The exact, general structure (any noise, any order).* By Bayes and the Markov
graph — the window $\mathbf z_{<t}$ is the Markov blanket on the past, so given it
$z_t\perp x_{1:t-1}$:
\[
\boxed{\;
p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})\;\propto\;
\underbrace{p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})}_{\text{transition (prediction)}}\;\cdot\;
\underbrace{p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)}_{\text{backward (evidence) message}}
\;}
\]
and, taking $\nabla_{z_t}\log$ (the normalizer is independent of $z_t$):
\[
\boxed{\;
\nabla\log p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})
= \underbrace{\nabla\log p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})}_{\text{transition score}}
+ \underbrace{\nabla\log p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)}_{\text{evidence score}}.
\;}
\]
*Only the prediction channel sees the order.* The backward message
$\beta_t(z_t)=p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)$ is /history-free/ (future obs are downstream of
$z_t$, independent of $\mathbf z_{<t}$), so the evidence machinery is identical for
every $j$. The transition score is what the diffusion already learns,
$s_\psi(z_t,\tau{\to}0,\mathbf z_{<t})\approx\nabla\log p_\psi^{\mathrm{sde}}(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})$.

*The Gaussian encoder is a Laplace / concentration approximation to that*
(Section [[*Why a Gaussian encoder when nothing else is Gaussian]]), exact only in the
linear-Gaussian (Kalman) limit (Section [[*The linear-Gaussian limit: the (extended) Kalman smoother]]).

*Recommended encoder: persistence-framed blend* ([[*The encoder (persistence-framed blend)]]). Frame the
mean on the most recent latent and correct it by a /z-free evidence estimate/
through a /learned gate/; fix the posterior-std scale $\sigma_0=1$:
\[
\boxed{\;
\mu_t = z_{t-1} + K_t\odot(m_t^b - z_{t-1}),\qquad
K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U + b_K),\qquad
\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t = W_\sigma U + b_\sigma\ \ (\sigma_0{=}1).
\;}
\]
Two named modules build the features (Section [[*The two mixers]]):
- *Time Mixer* — mixes the $j$ lags of $\mathbf z_{<t}$ (per site) into a history
  feature; its linear read-out /is/ the baseline $\mu_p$ (default = persistence =
  select $z_{t-1}$).
- *Feature Mixer* — mixes the $N$ sites (the renamed spatial `Mix`); coordinate-aware.

$m_t^b$ is the z-free evidence mean (from the evidence trunk $E$: cross-attention to
$x_{t:T}$ $+$ Feature Mixer); $U$ is the state-conditioned feature (Time Mixer over
$\mathbf z_{<t}$, then cross-attention $+$ Feature Mixer). The gate $K_t\in(0,1)^D$
interpolates "persist at $z_{t-1}$" and "go to the evidence." Three benefits, all
free:
- /Cleanest conditioning./ Persistence frame $\Rightarrow$ the dominant recurrent
  Jacobian is $\partial\mu_t/\partial z_{t-1}=(I-K_t)$ — a contraction toward $0$; no
  learned dynamics map.
- /Zero-init handoff./ $b_K\ll0$, $b_\sigma=0\Rightarrow q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$
  (the random-walk prior); KL$\,\approx0$ at the stage-1$\to$2 handoff.
- /Unit-variance convention./ Fixing $\sigma_0=1$ pressures the latent's increments
  to $\approx$ unit scale — what EDM's $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}\approx1$ wants.

*Why persistence is the default* ([[*The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline]]). A learned
baseline $\mu_p$ (a nontrivial Time Mixer) is mostly an /efficiency/ gain, not a
capability one — the conditional diffusion (and, under strong evidence, $m_t^b$)
already represent any drift. Persistence is /free/ (parameter-free; strictly better
than the /zero/ baseline $=$ model the increment) and avoids the learned baseline's
parameters, pretraining, and pinned/learnable bookkeeping. Opt into an
extrapolation / Linear-AR$(j)$ / MLP Time-Mix baseline only when you /measure/ you
need it.

* The object to amortize: the smoother conditional

** Why a conditional, not a marginal

The optimal variational posterior for an order-$j$ Markov SSM factorizes as
\[
q_\phi(z_{1:T}\mid x) = q_\phi(z_{1:j}\mid x)\prod_{t>j} q_\phi(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T}),
\]
(the $j=1$ form is used throughout [[file:model-v2.org::*Model][model-v2]], made exact in the ELBO by the
marginalization lemma in [[file:model-v2.org::*ELBO modifications][ELBO modifications]]). The encoder models the /conditional
kernel/ of the posterior Markov chain — /not/ the smoother marginal
$p(z_t\mid x_{1:T})$. The conditioning set is $x_{t:T}$: the window $\mathbf z_{<t}$
is the Markov blanket on the past, so $p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{1:T})=p(z_t\mid
\mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})$; the realized window is a sufficient statistic for the
entire past.

This distinction matters twice below: the marginal smoother (RTS) is /not/ the right
object to copy, and conditioning on $\mathbf z_{<t}$ is precisely what makes a
Gaussian encoder defensible ([[*Temporal conditioning tightens the target]]).

** The general factorization (exact, any noise)

By Bayes, using $x_{t:T}\perp \mathbf z_{<t}\mid z_t$ (future obs are downstream of
$z_t$):
\[
p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})
\;\propto\; p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})\;p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)
\;=\; \underbrace{p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})}_{\text{forward / transition}}\;\times\;
\underbrace{\beta_t(z_t)}_{\text{backward message}},
\]
$\beta_t(z_t):=p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)$. /No distributional assumption is made/: this is
the Krishnan "two-channel fusion" (prediction $\otimes$ evidence) for an arbitrary
order-$j$ transition and emission. It holds verbatim when $p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})$
is your diffusion transition.

** Score-additive form (exact, any noise)

Taking $\nabla_{z_t}\log$,
\[
\nabla\log p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})
= \nabla\log p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t}) + \nabla\log\beta_t(z_t).
\]
1. /Exact for any noise and order/ — the gain formula of the LG limit is the special
   case where both terms are /linear/ in $z_t$.
2. /The transition score is already modeled/: it is the $\tau{\to}0$ limit of the
   generative score net $s_\psi(\cdot,\tau,\mathbf z_{<t})$, which simply takes the
   window in its conditioning slot. (A /tempting/ deeper design — the encoder emits a
   learned evidence score added to $s_\psi$ — would make the encoder marginal
   non-Gaussian and forfeit the closed-form ESM target $s_q$ of
   [[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][model-v2]]; we keep the encoder Gaussian and only /borrow/ the structure.)

** The backward message is order-agnostic

$\beta_t$ obeys a backward recursion ($T\to 1$):
\[
\beta_t(z_t) = p(x_t\mid z_t)\int p(z_{t+1}\mid z_t,\dots)\,\beta_{t+1}(z_{t+1})\,\cdots\,dz_{t+1}\cdots,
\]
and is a function of $z_t$ only — /never the history $\mathbf z_{<t}$/. This is why
the evidence channel (the trunk $E$ and $m_t^b$) is identical for every $j$: the
Markov order touches only the prediction channel. The future-aware backbone $h_t$
(bidirectional / backward-causal) is the amortized stand-in for $\beta_t$, the
sufficient summary of $x_{t:T}$.

* Why a Gaussian encoder when nothing else is Gaussian

The SSM is non-Gaussian, yet we output $q_\phi=\mathcal N(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2)$.
This is justified as an approximation, plus required by our machinery.

** Laplace / concentration view

Write the true conditional as $p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{t:T})\propto e^{\ell(z_t)}$,
$\ell(z_t)=\log p(z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t})+\log\beta_t(z_t)$. A second-order expansion
about the mode gives the Laplace approximation
$\mathcal N(z_t^\star,[-\nabla^2\ell]^{-1})$; the encoder amortizes (a variational
version of) it. Error is controlled by third+ derivatives relative to curvature —
/small whenever the conditional is concentrated and unimodal/. The EKS is this
approximation with both factors linearized, so "Gaussian encoder" and "EKS" are the
same modeling assumption at different fidelity.

** Temporal conditioning tightens the target

Conditioning on the realized window buys the concentration. By the law of total
variance,
\[
\mathbb E_{\mathbf z_{<t}}\!\bigl[\mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{1:T}]\bigr]
= \mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid x_{1:T}] - \mathrm{Var}_{\mathbf z_{<t}}\!\bigl(\mathbb E[z_t\mid \mathbf z_{<t},x_{1:T}]\bigr)
\;\le\; \mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid x_{1:T}],
\]
so the conditional is /tighter than the smoothing marginal/, by the spread of the
conditional mean — and a larger window (bigger $j$) can only tighten it further.
A tighter, more concentrated target is better approximated by a Gaussian: the
structured temporal posterior is a /friendlier/ setting than a static VAE's marginal
(the model-v2 $q$-Gaussian question, [[file:model-v2.org::*ESM Formulation][ESM Formulation]] TODO).

** Failure mode: multimodal transitions

A diffusion transition /can/ be genuinely multimodal (bifurcations); then the product
is multimodal, a Gaussian $q$ cannot represent it, and the ELBO gap is real. For
deterministic-/chaotic/ dynamics with small forcing — our regime — the transition is
sharply unimodal around its prediction and the Gaussian encoder is sound. Diagnose
with posterior-predictive multimodality.

** It is also required by the ESM machinery

The closed-form ESM target score $s_q$ ([[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][Closed-form target score]]) exists /because/ $q$
is Gaussian (a Gaussian convolved with the linear diffusion kernel stays Gaussian).
A non-Gaussian encoder would force denoising-score-matching and reintroduce the
variance we are removing. So Gaussian-$q$ is both a good approximation and the price
of the closed-form target.

* The linear-Gaussian limit: the (extended) Kalman smoother

The /special case/ that motivates the gain parameterization — and answers "what is
an EKS?" — but is *not* the model. Order-$j$ linear-Gaussian SSM:
\[
z_t = \textstyle\sum_{k=1}^{j} A_k z_{t-k} + b + w_t,\quad w_t\sim\mathcal N(0,Q),\qquad
x_t = e(z_t) + v_t,\quad v_t\sim\mathcal N(0,R).
\]
The transition mean $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})=\sum_k A_k z_{t-k}+b$ is a /linear Time
Mix/ of the window; the recurrence is governed by the *companion matrix* of
$\{A_k\}$.

** EKF / EKS reference (the marginal)

Stack the window into a state $\bar z_t=(z_t,\dots,z_{t-j+1})$ so the system is
first-order in $\bar z$ with companion transition; then it is the ordinary KF/RTS in
$\bar z$. /EKF predict/ uses $m_t^- = \sum_k A_k m_{t-k}$; /update/ folds in $x_t$;
/RTS/ recurses backward. This yields the marginal $p(z_t\mid x_{1:T})$ — /not/ the
encoder's object ([[*Why a conditional, not a marginal]]).

** The conditional in the LG limit (the gain form)

Specialize the factorization with both factors Gaussian:
$p(z_t\mid\mathbf z_{<t})=\mathcal N(\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t}),Q)$ and
$\beta_t=\mathcal N(m_t^b,P_t^b)$. The product is Gaussian, $q=\mathcal N(\mu_t,\Sigma_t)$:
\[
K_t = Q(Q+P_t^b)^{-1},\qquad
\mu_t = \mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t}) + K_t\bigl(m_t^b - \mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})\bigr),\qquad
\Sigma_t = (I-K_t)Q,
\]
the /score-additive identity made concrete/ (linear scores $\Rightarrow$ precisions
add $\Rightarrow$ a gain on means). In the diagonal case the gain is a logistic gate
of log-precisions,
$K_{t,d}=\sigma_{p,d}^2/(\sigma_{p,d}^2+P_{t,d}^b)=\mathrm{sigmoid}(\log\sigma_{p,d}^2-\log P_{t,d}^b)$
— the shape a /learned-baseline/ encoder's gate generalizes; under the persistence
default with $\sigma_0{=}1$ it degenerates to $\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$
([[*The encoder (persistence-framed blend)]]). The prediction $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})$ is /exact/ even here
(we condition on the realized window); the "extended" approximation enters only
through forcing $\beta_t$ Gaussian.

** What the limit does and doesn't tell us

- /Does:/ the /shape/ of a good Gaussian parameterization — center on the prediction
  (a Time Mix of the window), correct by a gated evidence term — and the limiting
  values ($K_t{=}0\Rightarrow q{=}$prior).
- /Doesn't:/ the closed forms $K_t=Q(Q+P^b)^{-1}$, $\Sigma_t=(I-K_t)Q$, and the exact
  Jacobian are LG-only. We do /not/ freeze them — we /learn/ the gate and earn the
  conditioning by /construction/ (next section).

* The encoder (persistence-framed blend)

** Architecture

Persistence baseline ($\mu_p=z_{t-1}$, the trivial Time Mix) and fixed scale
$\sigma_0=1$, so /no prior heads/. The *evidence trunk $E$ reads $x_{t:T}$ only* and
produces a /z-free, history-free/ evidence mean $m_t^b$; the *state feature $U$*
ingests the window through the Time Mixer:
\begin{align}
m_t^b &= \text{z-free evidence mean (trunk } E;\ \text{see [[*What to build]])}, \\
U &= E + \mathrm{TimeMixer}(\mathbf z_{<t}), \\
K_t &= \mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U + b_K)\ \in(0,1)^D, \\
\mu_t &= z_{t-1} + K_t\odot(m_t^b - z_{t-1}), \\
\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t &= W_\sigma U + b_\sigma,
\end{align}
\[
z_t = \mu_t + \boldsymbol\sigma_t\odot\eta_t,\quad \eta_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I),
\]
with $b_K\ll0$ (gate closed) and $b_\sigma=0$ ($\sigma_0=1$) at init, so
$q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ — the random-walk prior — at the handoff. The older
lags $z_{t-2:t-j}$ enter only through $\mathrm{TimeMixer}$ into $U$ (hence the gate
and std); the /frame/ stays $z_{t-1}$ (persistence). Replacing $z_{t-1}$ by a learned
Time-Mix baseline $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})$ is the opt-in upgrade
([[*The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline]]).

This is the blend ([[*Blend vs scale (decided: blend)]]) at $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$, $\boldsymbol\sigma_p=1$;
the general gain form's prior-variance gate feature and variance frame vanish
($\log\sigma_p^2=0$). The gate still sees the whole window — through $U$ — only the
evidence /estimate/ $m_t^b$ must be z-free (next).

** Properties (all hold for arbitrary noise — they are construction facts)

*Cleanest recurrent Jacobian.* With the persistence frame and $m_t^b$ z-free, the
dominant lag-1 Jacobian is
\[
\frac{\partial\mu_t}{\partial z_{t-1}} = (I-K_t) + (\partial K_t/\partial z_{t-1})\odot(m_t^b-z_{t-1}),
\qquad
\frac{\partial\mu_t}{\partial z_{t-k}} = (\partial K_t/\partial z_{t-k})\odot(m_t^b-z_{t-1})\ \ (k>1),
\]
i.e. the older lags enter $\mu_t$ /only/ through the gate (a controlled term, $\approx0$
at init). The lag-1 term is a per-dim contraction ($K_t\in(0,1)^D$), $\to0$ where the
future is informative. There is /no learned dynamics map to expand/; the
through-time companion product is governed by these contracted blocks
([[*Why the gain form conditions well]]).

*Zero-init handoff.* $b_K\ll0,\,b_\sigma=0\Rightarrow\mu_t\approx z_{t-1}$,
$\boldsymbol\sigma_t=1$, i.e. $q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$, so the transition KL
starts $\approx0$.

*Interpretability.* $K_t$ = "trust the future evidence vs. persist," per dimension.

** Sampling algorithm (encoder draw $z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)$)

#+begin_src text
h_{1:T} = Backbone(x_{1:T})                       # parallel, future-aware (amortized β_t)
draw z_{1:j} from the initial encoder
for t = j+1 .. T:                                 # SEQUENTIAL (μ_t needs realized window)
    m_b   = EvidenceMean(E)                        # z-free, history-free (trunk E)
    hzt   = TimeMixer(z_{t-1}, ..., z_{t-j})       # mix the j lags -> history feature
    U     = E + hzt;  U = FeatureMixer(U, P)       # mix sites; (+ cross-attn refinement)
    K_t   = sigmoid(W_K U + b_K)                   # gate ∈ (0,1)^D; b_K ≪ 0
    μ_t   = z_{t-1} + K_t ⊙ (m_b − z_{t-1})        # persistence frame + gated innovation
    log σ_t = W_σ U + b_σ                          # b_σ = 0 → σ_t = 1 at init
    η_t ~ N(0,I);  z_t = μ_t + σ_t ⊙ η_t
return z_{1:T}
#+end_src

Latency $\Theta(T)$ sequential; the window is available as it is realized.

* The two mixers

The backbone-side feature processing factors into two swappable modules over two
axes — /time/ (the $j$ lags) and /sites/ (the $N$ spatial units). Everything else
(the frame, gate, std heads, evidence cross-attention) is fixed.

** Time Mixer (over the $j$ lags)

$\mathrm{TimeMixer}:(\mathbb R^{j\times N\times C_z})\to\mathbb R^{N\times C}$ mixes the
window's lag axis (per site) into a history feature. /It also supplies the baseline/:
its linear read-out to latent space is the prediction $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})$.

- /Default (persistence)/: select the last lag, $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$ — a trivial Time Mix
  (the older lags still feed $U$, just not the frame). Parameter-free.
- /Extrapolation/: a fixed linear Time Mix — e.g. $2z_{t-1}-z_{t-2}$ (constant
  velocity, $j{=}2$), $3z_{t-1}-3z_{t-2}+z_{t-3}$ (constant acceleration, $j{=}3$).
- /Linear-AR$(j)$/: a /learned/ linear Time Mix $\sum_k A_k z_{t-k}+b$.
- /MLP/: a nonlinear Time Mix of the window.

Implementations: concat-then-MLP over the $j$ lags, a depthwise temporal conv, or a
small causal attention over the lag axis. The baseline knob ([[*The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline]])
/is/ the choice of Time Mixer.

** Feature Mixer (over the $N$ sites)

$\mathrm{FeatureMixer}:(\mathbb R^{N\times C},P)\to\mathbb R^{N\times C}$ couples the $N$
sites among themselves (the sites are the multivariate "features" of the field —
pixels / sensors / graph nodes — hence the name; this is the renamed spatial `Mix`,
the latent-field operator). Cross-attention only links sites to /observations/; the
Feature Mixer links sites to /each other/. Default — pre-LN site self-attention, made
coordinate-aware through $\gamma(P)$:
\[
\mathrm{FeatureMixer}(U) = U + \mathrm{MHSelfAttn}\!\bigl(q{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)+\gamma(P),\ k{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)+\gamma(P),\ v{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)\bigr),
\]
followed by a feed-forward residual $U\leftarrow U+\mathrm{FFN}(\mathrm{LN}(U))$. It is
permutation-equivariant, coordinate-aware, and handles variable $N$ — the /same/
block works on any point set (grid, mesh, graph nodes, channels). Cost $O(N^2C)$ per
block; for large $N$ swap a sub-quadratic Feature Mixer (windowed/axial attention, a
Perceiver $N\!\to\!M\!\to\!N$ bottleneck, or k-NN graph attention) behind the same
interface. In the z-free trunk $E$ the Feature Mixer reads only $(E,P)$ — no state, no
history — so $m_t^b$ stays z-free.

** Per-dataset menu

| data | sites $N$ (Feature Mixer) | order $j$ / Time Mixer | $\gamma$ |
|------+----------------------------+------------------------+----------|
| *EEG* | ~64 channels — full self-attn | $j{=}2$, Linear-AR(2) (oscillation) | electrode-pos / learned |
| *Video* | large grid — windowed/axial or Perceiver | $j{=}2{-}3$, persistence or extrapolation | 2D Fourier |
| *Wildfire* | grid/mesh — local attn or GNO | persistence (try Linear) | 2D Fourier |
| *Traffic* | road graph — graph attention | persistence (try Linear) | Laplacian PE |

* The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline

The frame $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})$ is the Time Mixer's prediction — the model-v2
/Baseline-form/ knob ([[file:model-v2.org::*Baseline-form variants][Baseline-form variants]]), now over the window: Zero, Persistence,
Extrapolation, Linear-AR$(j)$, MLP. We default to *Persistence* ($z_{t-1}$); the rest
are opt-in. (All span the /same reachable Gaussian family/ — with $m_t^b$ free the
blend reaches any mean — so this is conditioning/init/bias, not expressiveness.)

** Why persistence, not a learned baseline

Not about representability: the conditional diffusion $p_\psi(z_t\mid\mathbf z_{<t})$
learns any drift (the $\mu_p$-degeneracy: shift $\mu_p$ by $\Delta$ and the diffusion
residual by $-\Delta$ — same model), and under strong evidence $m_t^b$ carries it too.
A learned baseline buys /efficiency/ ($\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ at noise rather than
signal scale, smaller residual, better init) and /weak-evidence robustness/ (where
$K_t\to0$, $\mu_t\to\mu_p$) — not capability. Against that, persistence is /free over
Zero/ (modeling the increment beats modeling $z_t$ from scratch), gives the cleanest
conditioning $(I-K_t)$, and deletes the baseline's parameters, pretraining,
pinned/learnable/anchor knobs and the $\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ machinery (so model-v2's
"$\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ discarded after handoff" is /true again/). Start at
persistence; earn $\mu_p$.

** When to upgrade (and to what)

Add a baseline only on the /symptom/: $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ at signal scale, poor
samples on clean-drift data, or weak-evidence/forecasting. Diagnose by the companion
spectral radius of the learned drift and the increment statistics
$\mathbb E\|z_t-z_{t-1}\|$ vs $\mathbb E\|z_t\|$. Reach for *Extrapolation* /
*Linear-AR$(j)$* before *MLP* — they capture the cases that need a baseline (rotation,
advection, oscillation) as a single (fixed or learned) linear Time Mix, with none of
the MLP's pretraining fragility. The value of an /expressive/ baseline also shrinks
with multimodality (a point can only capture the mean, a low-density valley).

*EEG is the canonical higher-order case.* A sinusoid satisfies
$z_t = 2\cos(\omega\Delta t)\,z_{t-1} - z_{t-2}$ /exactly/ — oscillation is intrinsically
order-2. So EEG wants $j{=}2$ with a *Linear-AR(2)* Time-Mix baseline (the free
constant-velocity extrapolation $2z_{t-1}-z_{t-2}$ is the slow-$\omega$ / fine-$\Delta t$
approximation). The persistence-dominated fields (video, wildfire, traffic) stay at
persistence; their unpredictable part is multimodal, so a point baseline is a poor
summary and the diffusion $+$ evidence carry it. It is the /latent/ transition and
$\Delta t$ that decide — measure, don't assume.

** Endpoints and model-v2 modes

- *C* (current) $=$ /Zero/ baseline with a free combiner: recurrent Jacobian unpinned
  ($\rho^{t-s}$ blow-up).
- *A* $=$ /Identity/ baseline (persistence). Bare A ($\mu_t=z_{t-1}+g_\phi$, free
  $g_\phi$) tames $J\approx I$ but mis-centers under real drift; /our/ encoder is A in
  /blend/ form (z-free $m_t^b$ $+$ gate), adding the $(I-K_t)$ damping and the clean
  init.
- The /Baseline-mode/ knob ([[file:model-v2.org::*Baseline-mode variants][Baseline-mode variants]]) — pinned vs. learnable-with-anchor,
  shared vs. own-copy — only bites once $\mu_p$ has parameters; for persistence it is
  degenerate (pinned). ($\psi$-gradients are benign throughout.)

* Why the gain form conditions well

** Gradient conditioning (a construction fact, not a Gaussian claim)

Reparametrize ($\eta$ fixed); the encoder mean reads the realized window, so the
$\phi$-gradient propagates through a /companion/ recurrence whose blocks are the
lag-Jacobians $J_t^{(k)}=\partial\mu_t/\partial z_{t-k}$. With persistence and z-free
$m_t^b$, $J_t^{(1)}=(I-K_t)+\text{(gate)}$ and $J_t^{(k>1)}=\text{(gate only)}$ — all
contracted by the gate and $\approx0$ at init, so the through-time product stays
controlled /whatever the SSM noise is/. This is the real fix for C's blow-up; it does
not rely on the conditional being Gaussian (only on how we wrote $\mu_t$).

** Initialization and inductive bias

Gate-closed init $\Rightarrow q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$, recurrence at the
identity companion — the best-conditioned start. The bias is "/persist, then correct
by evidence/" — the random-walk frame made well-conditioned by the gate, without C's
unanchored freedom. Variance is free with $\sigma_0=1$ ([[*Variance: free vs coupled]]); the gate
drives only the mean.

** Relation to the true (non-Gaussian) conditional

The encoder targets the /general/ conditional $\propto p(z_t\mid\mathbf z_{<t})\beta_t$,
of which the gain form is the LG-limit exact case and, away from it, a
well-conditioned Gaussian /inductive bias/ — prediction $+$ gated evidence,
evidence-vanishing at init — without pretending the closed-form gain is the truth.

* Design knobs and open questions

** Blend vs scale (decided: blend)

The mean correction has two algebraically identical writings ($\delta_t=m_t^b-z_{t-1}$):
/scale/ $\mu_t=z_{t-1}+K_t\odot\delta_t$ and /blend/ $\mu_t=(1-K_t)z_{t-1}+K_t\odot m_t^b$.
With persistence they diverge once the evidence read-out is constrained:
- *Blend, $m_t^b$ z-free*: $\partial\mu_t/\partial z_{t-1}=(I-K_t)+(\partial K_t)(m_t^b-z_{t-1})$
  — a contraction /below/ $I$; where the future is informative ($K_t\to1$),
  $z_{t-1}$-sensitivity $\to0$ (the smoother property).
- *Scale, $\delta_t$ z-dependent*: $\partial\mu_t/\partial z_{t-1}=I+\partial(K_t\odot\delta_t)/\partial z_{t-1}$
  — pinned at $\approx I$, can be pushed /above/ $I$; evidence cannot shut the
  recurrence off.
(At $K_t\to1$: $\|J_{\mathrm{blend}}\|\to0$ vs $\|J_{\mathrm{scale}}\|\gtrsim1$.)

*Decision: blend with z-free $m_t^b$.* It lets informative evidence contract the
recurrence below the identity — valuable whenever the future disambiguates the state,
and a margin even if the latent dynamics turn expansive. Only the evidence /estimate/
$m_t^b$ is z-free; the gate $K_t$ still sees the window via $U$. Cost: the
coordinate-queried evidence trunk $E$ ([[*What to build]]). /When scale is fine:/ if the
evidence is weak everywhere ($K_t\approx0$), the single feature suffices and is
cheaper.

** Diagonal vs full gain

The gate is diagonal (a per-dim sigmoid, $K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$), so
$K_t\in(0,1)^D$ automatically. Three reasons not to go full:
1. /Closed-form pipeline./ A full gate breaks the per-dim ESM/EDM closed forms
   ([[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][Closed-form target score]]): the target score would need a matrix inverse. The
   diagonal posterior is the load-bearing commitment.
2. /Redundant for the mean./ $m_t^b$ already mixes dimensions (the Feature Mixer), so a
   full gate buys no reachable-mean expressiveness.
3. /Validity./ A diagonal sigmoid gate is automatically a contraction
   $0\prec K_t\prec I$; a naive full gate (elementwise sigmoid on a matrix) need not
   be — e.g. $K=\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}0&.9\\.9&0\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$ has
   $\|I-K\|=1.9>1$; enforcing $0\preceq K\preceq I$ needs an expensive parameterization.
/Open:/ low-rank-plus-diagonal gain if cross-dim smoothing matters.

** Variance: free vs coupled

With persistence we use /free/ variance $\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t=W_\sigma U+b_\sigma$,
$b_\sigma=0$ so $\sigma_0=1$ at init — no Gaussian variance law imposed; $W_\sigma U$
sets the per-state / per-$t$ spread. The /coupled/ law
$\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2=(1-K_t)\boldsymbol\sigma_p^2$ needs a $\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ head,
i.e. it is only available with a learned baseline ([[*The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline]]); even then it
is an ablation (the variance law $(1-K_t)$ is Gaussian-specific).

** Note on $\sigma_0$ vs $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$

$\sigma_0=1$ is the encoder's posterior-std /init scale/ (the bias of the log-std
head). $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}(t)$ is the /data spread/ of the increment, used by EDM,
and is /larger/ — $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}^2=\mathbb E[\sigma_t^2]+\mathrm{Var}[\hat\mu_t]$
includes the spread of the predicted innovations. They are different objects;
$\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ is tracked/fixed separately for EDM and is /not/ $\sigma_0$.

** Residual framing is ELBO-invariant

Centering both the generative model and the variational posterior on the same frame
(persistence, or any $\mu_p$) is an invertible volume-preserving reparameterization of
$z_t$, applied identically to $q$ and $p$, so the KL — hence the ELBO — is
/numerically unchanged/: $\mathrm{KL}[q(z_t\mid\cdot)\|p(z_t\mid\cdot)]=\mathrm{KL}[\tilde q(\delta_t\mid\cdot)\|\tilde p(\delta_t\mid\cdot)]$,
$\delta_t=z_t-\mu_p$. What it buys is /conditioning/: in the score-matching residual
$s_q-s_\psi$ the shared frame cancels, so the objective regresses the /increment/
(target $=$ the gated innovation, $\to0$ at init), with the window entering only as a
conditioning input. This is why the whole baseline discussion is about conditioning,
never the bound.

** Should the gate see the history?

Yes — through $U$. The evidence /estimate/ $m_t^b$ is z-free and history-free (that is
what gives the damping). The gate $K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$ reads $U$ (evidence
$+$ the Time-Mixed window), so it sees the whole window — it sets /how much/ evidence
to trust /and where to look/. This enters only the controlled $\partial K_t$ Jacobian
terms, never the dominant $(I-K_t)$ ([[*Properties (all hold for arbitrary noise — they are construction facts)][Properties]]).

* What to build

A concrete grid-free realization: the *persistence-framed blend* over the $j$-window,
with a *z-free evidence estimate*, fixed $\sigma_0=1$, and the *Time Mixer* /
*Feature Mixer* modules. Latents on $N$ sites with coordinates $P$; observations
summarized by $h_t$ at coordinates $Q$. No prior heads.

#+begin_src text
# 1. z-FREE evidence trunk E -> m^b   (no state, no history => ∂m^b/∂z = 0 => damping)
E = γ(P)                                          # [N,C]  query = coordinates only
for l = 1..L_e:
    E = E + CrossAttn(q = LN(E)+γ(P), kv = Wkv(h_t)+γ(Q))   # gather evidence (sites←obs)
    E = E + FeatureMixer(LN(E), P)                          # couple sites
m_b = W_m·LN(E)                                    # evidence mean m^b   (z-FREE, [N,C_z])

# 2. STATE feature U: Time-Mix the window, then evidence + site mixing
hz  = TimeMixer(z_{t-1}, ..., z_{t-j})            # mix j lags (per site) -> [N,C]
U   = E + hz
for l = 1..L_u:
    U = U + CrossAttn(q = LN(U)+γ(P), kv = Wkv(h_t)+γ(Q))   # state-conditioned gather
    U = U + FeatureMixer(LN(U), P)
K_t = sigmoid(W_K·LN(U) + b_K)                    # gate ∈ (0,1)^D; b_K ≪ 0
s_t = W_σ·LN(U) + b_σ                             # log-std; b_σ = 0  (σ_0 = 1)

# 3. persistence-framed blend, then sample
μ_t = z_{t-1} + K_t ⊙ (m_b − z_{t-1})            # = (1−K_t)⊙z_{t-1} + K_t⊙m_b
σ_t = exp(s_t)                                    # = 1 at init
z_t = μ_t + σ_t ⊙ η_t
#+end_src

Key points:
- /Two mixers, two axes./ $\mathrm{TimeMixer}$ mixes the $j$ lags (and supplies the
  baseline; default persistence = select $z_{t-1}$); $\mathrm{FeatureMixer}$ mixes the
  $N$ sites. Both are swappable ([[*The two mixers]]); cross-attention gathers evidence.
- /Persistence frame, $\sigma_0=1$./ Gate closed at init ($b_K\ll0$) $\Rightarrow
  q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$, KL$\,\approx0$ at handoff. No prior heads.
- /z-free $m_t^b$ $\Rightarrow$ damping./ $m_t^b$ reads $E$ (coordinates only, no state,
  no history), so $\partial m_t^b/\partial z=0$ and the lag-1 Jacobian is $(I-K_t)+\text{(gate)}$
  — a contraction below $I$ ([[*Blend vs scale (decided: blend)]]).
- /Gate/std see the full window./ $K_t,s_t$ read $U$ (= $E$ $+$ Time-Mixed window, then
  cross-attn $+$ Feature Mixer), so the state/history controls /how much/ to trust
  evidence /and where to look/; only $m_t^b$ is z-free.
- /Free variance./ $s_t=W_\sigma U+b_\sigma$, $\sigma_0=1$ at init ([[*Variance: free vs coupled]]).
- /Downstream unchanged./ ELBO, the closed-form ESM target $s_q$, and EDM all consume
  $(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t)$ as before; $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ is tracked
  separately and is /not/ $\sigma_0$.
- /Opt-in baseline./ For a learned drift (e.g. EEG, $j{=}2$): make $\mathrm{TimeMixer}$
  produce $\mu_p(\mathbf z_{<t})$ (Linear-AR$(j)$ / MLP), frame on it, and reinstate the
  prior-variance gate feature; see [[*The baseline knob: persistence vs a learned baseline]].
