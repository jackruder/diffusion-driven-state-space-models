* Overview / TL;DR

The encoder amortizes the *smoother conditional* $q_\phi(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})
= \mathcal N(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2)$. The Gaussian here is a /variational
output form/, *not* an assumption about the SSM: the generative transition is a
diffusion and the emission a decoder, so the true noise — hence the true
conditional — can be arbitrary / nonparametric and non-Gaussian.

*The exact, general structure (any noise).* By Bayes and the Markov graph
(no Gaussianity used anywhere):
\[
\boxed{\;
p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})\;\propto\;
\underbrace{p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})}_{\text{transition (prediction)}}\;\cdot\;
\underbrace{p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)}_{\text{backward (evidence) message}}
\;}
\]
and, taking $\nabla_{z_t}\log$ (the normalizer is independent of $z_t$):
\[
\boxed{\;
\nabla\log p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})
= \underbrace{\nabla\log p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})}_{\text{transition score}}
+ \underbrace{\nabla\log p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)}_{\text{evidence score}}.
\;}
\]
The /posterior score is the sum of the transition score and the future-evidence
score/. The first term is exactly what the generative diffusion already learns,
$s_\psi(z_t,\tau{\to}0,z_{t-1})\approx\nabla\log p_\psi^{\mathrm{sde}}(z_t\mid z_{t-1})$
(modulo the $\tau{\to}0$ boundary). So the true posterior is "the score the
diffusion already models, plus an evidence correction." /This/ — not any
Gaussian gain formula — is the true form.

*The Gaussian encoder is a Laplace / concentration approximation to that*
(Section [[*Why a Gaussian encoder when nothing else is Gaussian]]), exact only in the
linear-Gaussian (Kalman) limit (Section [[*The linear-Gaussian limit: the (extended) Kalman smoother]]).

*Recommended encoder: identity-framed blend* ([[*The encoder (identity-framed blend)]]). Frame the
mean on the previous latent and correct it by a /z-free evidence estimate/ through
a /learned gate/; fix the posterior-std scale $\sigma_0=1$:
\[
\boxed{\;
\mu_t = z_{t-1} + K_t\odot(m_t^b - z_{t-1}),\qquad
K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U + b_K),\qquad
\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t = W_\sigma U + b_\sigma\ \ (\sigma_0{=}1).
\;}
\]
$m_t^b$ is the z-free evidence mean (from a coordinate-queried backbone $E$); $U$ is
a state-conditioned feature (it sees $z_{t-1}$ and the evidence); the gate
$K_t\in(0,1)^D$ interpolates "stay at $z_{t-1}$" and "go to the evidence." It is the
/blend/ form with the /identity/ baseline. Three benefits, all free:

- /Cleanest conditioning./ Identity $\Rightarrow F_p=I$, so the recurrent Jacobian is
  $J_t=(I-K_t)+(\partial K_t)(m_t^b-z_{t-1})$ — a contraction toward $0$, with /no
  learned dynamics map that could expand/.
- /Zero-init handoff./ $b_K\ll0$, $b_\sigma=0\Rightarrow q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$
  (the random-walk prior); KL$\,\approx0$ at the stage-1$\to$2 handoff.
- /Unit-variance convention./ Fixing $\sigma_0=1$ pressures the latent's increments
  to $\approx$ unit scale — what EDM's $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}\approx1$ calibration
  wants.

*Why identity is the default* ([[*The baseline knob: identity vs a learned baseline]]). A learned
baseline $\mu_p$ is mostly an /efficiency/ gain, not a capability one — the
conditional diffusion (and, under strong evidence, $m_t^b$) already represent any
drift. Identity is /free/ (parameter-free; strictly better than the /zero/ baseline
$=$ model the increment) and avoids the Linear/MLP baseline's parameters,
pretraining, pinned/learnable/anchor knobs, and the state-conditional
$\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ machinery. Opt into a Linear (then MLP) baseline only when you
/measure/ you need it. The previously-debated encoders are the endpoints:

- *C* (current): /zero/ baseline, gate folded into a free combiner; recurrent
  Jacobian unpinned ($\rho^{t-s}$ blow-up).
- *A* $\approx$ /this/: /identity/ baseline — but the /blend/ form with z-free
  evidence and a gate adds the $(I-K_t)$ damping and the clean init that bare A
  lacked.
- /Linear / MLP $\mu_p$/: opt-in upgrades, justified only by a predictable
  structured drift ([[*The baseline knob: identity vs a learned baseline]]).

* The object to amortize: the smoother conditional

** Why a conditional, not a marginal

The optimal variational posterior for our Markov SSM factorizes as
\[
q_\phi(z_{1:T}\mid x) = q_\phi(z_1\mid x)\prod_{t=2}^T q_\phi(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T}),
\]
(used throughout [[file:model-v2.org::*Model][model-v2]], made exact in the ELBO by the marginalization lemma in
[[file:model-v2.org::*ELBO modifications][ELBO modifications]]). The encoder models the /conditional kernel/ of the posterior
Markov chain — /not/ the smoother marginal $p(z_t\mid x_{1:T})$. The conditioning
set is $x_{t:T}$: by the Markov structure, given $z_{t-1}$ the past $x_{1:t-1}$ is
d-separated from $z_t$, so $p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{1:T})=p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})$;
the realized $z_{t-1}$ is the sufficient statistic for the entire past.

This distinction will matter twice below: the marginal smoother (RTS) is /not/
the right object to copy, and conditioning on $z_{t-1}$ is precisely what makes a
Gaussian encoder defensible ([[*Temporal conditioning tightens the target]]).

** The general factorization (exact, any noise)

By Bayes, using $x_{t:T}\perp z_{t-1}\mid z_t$ (future observations are downstream
of $z_t$):
\[
p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})
\;\propto\; p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})\;p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)
\;=\; \underbrace{p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})}_{\text{forward / transition}}\;\times\;
\underbrace{\beta_t(z_t)}_{\text{backward message}},
\]
$\beta_t(z_t):=p(x_{t:T}\mid z_t)$. /No distributional assumption is made/: this is
the Krishnan "two-channel fusion" (prediction $\otimes$ evidence) for an
arbitrary transition and emission. It holds verbatim when $p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})$ is
your diffusion transition.

** Score-additive form (exact, any noise)

Taking $\nabla_{z_t}\log$ of the factorization,
\[
\nabla\log p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})
= \nabla\log p(z_t\mid z_{t-1}) + \nabla\log\beta_t(z_t).
\]
Two reasons this is the right level to think at:

1. /It is exact for any noise/ — the gain formula of the LG limit is just the
   special case where both terms are /linear/ in $z_t$ (so "adding scores"
   becomes "a gain on means"); see [[*The linear-Gaussian limit: the (extended) Kalman smoother]].
2. /The transition score is already modeled/: it is the $\tau{\to}0$ limit of the
   generative score net $s_\psi(\cdot,\tau,z_{t-1})$. The posterior thus equals
   "diffusion score $+$ evidence score." (A /tempting/ deeper design — have the
   encoder emit a learned evidence score added to $s_\psi$ — would make the
   encoder marginal non-Gaussian and forfeit the closed-form ESM target $s_q$ of
   [[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][model-v2]]; we therefore keep the encoder Gaussian and only /borrow/ this
   structure as inductive bias.)

** The backward message and the future-aware backbone

$\beta_t$ obeys a backward recursion ($T\to 1$):
\[
\beta_t(z_t) = p(x_t\mid z_t)\int p(z_{t+1}\mid z_t)\,\beta_{t+1}(z_{t+1})\,dz_{t+1}.
\]
For /general/ noise this message is an arbitrary function of $z_t$ (not Gaussian).
This recursion is /why the backbone $h_t$ must be future-aware/
(bidirectional / backward-causal): $h_t$ is the amortized stand-in for $\beta_t$,
the sufficient summary of $x_{t:T}$. In the LG limit $\beta_t$ collapses to a
Gaussian information pair $(\nu_t,\Omega_t)$; in general $h_t$ carries whatever
finite summary the architecture affords, and the evidence head reads it.

* Why a Gaussian encoder when nothing else is Gaussian

The SSM is non-Gaussian, yet we output $q_\phi=\mathcal N(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2)$.
This is justified as an approximation, plus required by our machinery.

** Laplace / concentration view

Write the true conditional as $p(z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{t:T})\propto e^{\ell(z_t)}$,
\[
\ell(z_t) = \log p(z_t\mid z_{t-1}) + \log\beta_t(z_t).
\]
A second-order expansion of $\ell$ about its mode $z_t^\star$ gives the Laplace
approximation $\mathcal N\bigl(z_t^\star,\,[-\nabla^2\ell(z_t^\star)]^{-1}\bigr)$;
the Gaussian encoder amortizes (a variational version of) this pair. The error is
controlled by the third- and higher-order derivatives of $\ell$ relative to its
curvature — /small whenever the conditional is concentrated and unimodal/. The
EKS is exactly this approximation with the two factors linearized (linearization
makes them Gaussian, hence $\ell$ quadratic, hence Laplace exact). So "Gaussian
encoder" and "EKS" are the same modeling assumption at different fidelity, not
two separate leaps.

(VI with $\mathrm{KL}[q\Vert p]$, which the ELBO uses, is mode-seeking rather than
curvature-matching, so the learned $\boldsymbol\sigma_t$ may be modestly
under-dispersed relative to Laplace; the justification — concentration plus
unimodality — is the same.)

** Temporal conditioning tightens the target

Conditioning on the realized $z_{t-1}$ is what buys the concentration. By the law
of total variance,
\[
\mathbb E_{z_{t-1}}\!\bigl[\mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{1:T}]\bigr]
= \mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid x_{1:T}] - \mathrm{Var}_{z_{t-1}}\!\bigl(\mathbb E[z_t\mid z_{t-1},x_{1:T}]\bigr)
\;\le\; \mathrm{Var}[z_t\mid x_{1:T}],
\]
so the conditional is /tighter on average than the smoothing marginal/
$p(z_t\mid x_{1:T})$, by exactly the spread of the conditional mean. The
transition $p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})$ is itself usually concentrated (process noise
$\ll$ marginal spread). A tighter, more concentrated target is better
approximated by a Gaussian — so the /structured temporal/ posterior is a
/friendlier/ setting for a Gaussian than a static VAE's marginal posterior. This
is the answer to the model-v2 question of whether $q$-Gaussian is better
justified in the temporal context ([[file:model-v2.org::*ESM Formulation][ESM Formulation]] TODO): yes, because each
factor need only be locally Gaussian around a $z_{t-1}$-pinned mode.

** Failure mode: multimodal transitions

The honest caveat: a diffusion transition /can/ be genuinely multimodal
(bifurcations, mixed regimes), and a non-injective emission can make $\beta_t$
multimodal in $z_t$. Then the product is multimodal, a Gaussian $q$ cannot
represent it, and the ELBO gap is real and irreducible by training. For
deterministic-/chaotic/ dynamics with small stochastic forcing — our target
regime — the transition is sharply unimodal around $f(z_{t-1})$ and the Gaussian
encoder is sound. Diagnose with posterior-predictive checks / multimodality of
residuals if in doubt.

** It is also required by the ESM machinery

Independently of approximation quality, the closed-form ESM target score $s_q$
([[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][Closed-form target score]]) exists /because/ $q$ is Gaussian (a Gaussian convolved
with the linear diffusion kernel stays Gaussian). A non-Gaussian encoder would
force denoising-score-matching (LSGM-style) and reintroduce the variance we are
trying to remove. So Gaussian-$q$ is both an approximation we argue is good and
the price of the closed-form target.

* The linear-Gaussian limit: the (extended) Kalman smoother

This section is the /special case/ that motivates the gain parameterization — and
answers "what is the form of an EKS?" — but is *not* the model. Linear-Gaussian
(or linearized) SSM:
\[
z_t = f(z_{t-1}) + w_t,\quad w_t\sim\mathcal N(0,Q),\qquad
x_t = e(z_t) + v_t,\quad v_t\sim\mathcal N(0,R),
\]
with Jacobians $F_t=\partial f/\partial z|_{z_{t-1}}$, $H_t=\partial e/\partial z$.
Marks: $\;^-$ prediction, no mark filter, $\;^s$ smoother.

** EKF / EKS reference (the marginal)

/EKF predict:/ $m_t^- = f(m_{t-1})$, $P_t^- = F_t P_{t-1}F_t^\top + Q$. /Update:/
$y_t = x_t - e(m_t^-)$, $S_t = H_t P_t^- H_t^\top + R$,
$K_t^{\mathrm{ekf}} = P_t^- H_t^\top S_t^{-1}$, $m_t = m_t^- + K_t^{\mathrm{ekf}}y_t$,
$P_t = (I-K_t^{\mathrm{ekf}}H_t)P_t^-$.

/RTS smoother/ ($t=T-1,\dots,1$): $G_t = P_t F_{t+1}^\top(P_{t+1}^-)^{-1}$,
$m_t^s = m_t + G_t(m_{t+1}^s - m_{t+1}^-)$,
$P_t^s = P_t + G_t(P_{t+1}^s - P_{t+1}^-)G_t^\top$. This is the marginal
$p(z_t\mid x_{1:T})$ — /not/ the encoder's object ([[*Why a conditional, not a marginal]]).

** The conditional in the LG limit (the gain form)

Specialize the general factorization with /both/ factors Gaussian:
$p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})=\mathcal N(f(z_{t-1}),Q)$ and $\beta_t=\mathcal N(m_t^b,P_t^b)$.
The product is Gaussian (verified equal to the information-form combination
numerically), with the /gain form/
\[
K_t = Q(Q+P_t^b)^{-1},\qquad
\mu_t = f(z_{t-1}) + K_t\bigl(m_t^b - f(z_{t-1})\bigr),\qquad
\Sigma_t = (I-K_t)Q,
\]
equivalently $\Sigma_t^{-1}=Q^{-1}+\Omega_t$, $\mu_t=\Sigma_t(Q^{-1}f(z_{t-1})+\nu_t)$.
The gain $K_t\in[0,I]$ interpolates prediction ($K_t{=}0$, no evidence) and
evidence ($K_t{=}I$). /This is the score-additive identity made concrete/: linear
scores $\Rightarrow$ precisions add $\Rightarrow$ a gain on means. In the diagonal
case this gain is a /logistic gate of log-precisions/,
\[
K_{t,d}=\frac{\sigma_{p,d}^2}{\sigma_{p,d}^2+P_{t,d}^b}
=\mathrm{sigmoid}\!\bigl(\log\sigma_{p,d}^2-\log P_{t,d}^b\bigr),
\]
i.e. a /logistic gate of log-precisions/ — the shape a /learned-baseline/ encoder's
gate generalizes (with $\log\sigma_p^2$ as a feature). Under the /identity/ default
($\sigma_0{=}1$, $\log\sigma_p^2{=}0$) this feature drops out and the gate degenerates
to $\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$ ([[*The encoder (identity-framed blend)]]).

Note the prediction $f(z_{t-1})$ is /exact/ even here (we condition on the
realized $z_{t-1}$, so there is no linearization of $f$ around an uncertain
previous state). The "extended" approximation enters /only/ through forcing
$\beta_t$ Gaussian.

** What the limit does and doesn't tell us

- /Does:/ it supplies the /shape/ of a good Gaussian parameterization — center on
  the prediction, correct by a gated evidence term — and the limiting values
  ($K_t{=}0\Rightarrow q{=}$prior).
- /Doesn't:/ the closed forms $K_t=Q(Q+P^b)^{-1}$, $\Sigma_t=(I-K_t)Q$, and the
  exact Jacobian $J_t=(I-K_t)F_t$ are LG-only. In your non-Gaussian model the
  true conditional has none of these in closed form. So we do /not/ freeze the gate
  to the Gaussian formula — we /learn/ it and earn the conditioning benefits by
  /construction/ (next section).

* The encoder (identity-framed blend)

** Architecture

Identity baseline ($\mu_p=z_{t-1}$) and fixed posterior-std scale $\sigma_0=1$, so
/no prior heads are needed/. The *evidence backbone reads $h_t$ only* (coordinate
query) and produces a /z-free/ evidence mean $m_t^b$; a /state-conditioned/ feature
$U$ (which sees $z_{t-1}$ and the evidence) drives the gate and std:
\begin{align}
m_t^b &= \text{z-free evidence mean (from } E;\ \text{see [[*What to build]])}, \\
K_t &= \mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U + b_K)\ \in(0,1)^D, \\
\mu_t &= z_{t-1} + K_t\odot(m_t^b - z_{t-1}) \;=\; (1-K_t)\odot z_{t-1} + K_t\odot m_t^b, \\
\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t &= W_\sigma U + b_\sigma,
\end{align}
\[
z_t = \mu_t + \boldsymbol\sigma_t\odot\eta_t,\quad \eta_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I),
\]
with $b_K\ll0$ (gate closed) and $b_\sigma=0$ ($\sigma_0=1$) at init, so
$q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ — the random-walk prior — at the handoff.

This is the blend ([[*Blend vs scale (decided: blend)]]) specialized to $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$,
$\boldsymbol\sigma_p=1$. The general gain form's prior-confidence feature
$\alpha\odot\log\boldsymbol\sigma_p^2$ and variance frame $\tfrac12\ell_p$ both
/vanish/ here ($\ell_p=\log1=0$) — that is why the gate reduces to
$\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$ and the std to $W_\sigma U+b_\sigma$. The gate still
sees $z_{t-1}$, through $U$; only the evidence /estimate/ $m_t^b$ must be z-free
(next).

** Properties (all hold for arbitrary noise — they are construction facts)

*Cleanest recurrent Jacobian.* With $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$ ($F_p=I$) and $m_t^b$ z-free,
\[
J_t = \frac{\partial\mu_t}{\partial z_{t-1}}
= \underbrace{(I-K_t)}_{\text{contraction}}
\;+\; \underbrace{(\partial K_t/\partial z_{t-1})\odot(m_t^b-z_{t-1})}_{\text{gain-sensitivity, controlled}}.
\]
The dominant term is a per-dim contraction ($K_t\in(0,1)^D\Rightarrow\|I-K_t\|\le1$),
vanishing where the future is informative ($K_t\to1$). There is /no learned $F_p$
to expand/ — the conditioning worry that motivated $\mu_p$ for chaotic latents is
gone. The second term ($\partial K_t/\partial z$ via $U$'s state-dependence) is
bounded and $\approx0$ at init. (Why z-free $m_t^b$: a z-dependent evidence
read-out would add $K_t\,\partial m_t^b/\partial z$ and lose the damping —
[[*Blend vs scale (decided: blend)]].)

*Zero-init handoff.* $b_K\ll0,\,b_\sigma=0\Rightarrow\mu_t\approx z_{t-1}$,
$\boldsymbol\sigma_t=1$, i.e. $q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ — the stage-1
random-walk prior — so the transition KL starts $\approx0$ (the $O(K_t)$ offset is
negligible).

*Interpretability.* $K_t$ reads as "trust the future evidence vs. persist," per
dimension.

** Sampling algorithm (encoder draw $z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)$)

#+begin_src text
h_{1:T} = Backbone(x_{1:T})                       # parallel, future-aware (amortized β_t)
η_1 ~ N(0,I); (μ_1,σ_1) from h_1;  z_1 = μ_1 + σ_1 ⊙ η_1
for t = 2 .. T:                                   # SEQUENTIAL (μ_t needs realized z_{t-1})
    m_b      = EvidenceMean(E)                    # z-free (coordinate-queried backbone)
    U        = StateHead(z_{t-1}, h_t)            # state-conditioned feature
    K_t      = sigmoid(W_K U + b_K)               # gate ∈ (0,1)^D; b_K ≪ 0 init
    μ_t      = z_{t-1} + K_t ⊙ (m_b − z_{t-1})    # identity frame + gated innovation
    log σ_t  = W_σ U + b_σ                        # b_σ = 0 → σ_t = 1 at init
    η_t ~ N(0,I);  z_t = μ_t + σ_t ⊙ η_t
return z_{1:T}
#+end_src

Latency $\Theta(T)$ sequential — identical to A/C.

* The baseline knob: identity vs a learned baseline

The mean frame $\mu_t=\mu_p(z_{t-1})+(\text{gated evidence})$ leaves $\mu_p$ a free
choice — the model-v2 /Baseline-form/ knob ([[file:model-v2.org::*Baseline-form variants][Baseline-form variants]]): Zero, Identity,
Linear, MLP. We default to *Identity*; the rest are opt-in. (All forms span the
/same reachable Gaussian family/ — with $m_t^b$ free the blend reaches any mean —
so this is about conditioning, init, and inductive bias, not expressiveness.)

** Why identity, not a learned $\mu_p$

It is /not/ about representability: the conditional diffusion
$p_\psi(z_t\mid z_{t-1})$ learns any drift (the $\mu_p$-degeneracy: shift $\mu_p$ by
$\Delta$ and the diffusion residual by $-\Delta$ — same model), and under strong
evidence the z-free $m_t^b$ carries it too. A learned $\mu_p$ buys /efficiency/
(smaller residual, $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ at the noise rather than the signal
scale, better init) and /weak-evidence robustness/ (where $K_t\to0$, $\mu_t\to\mu_p$,
so a good prediction matters) — not capability.

Against that, identity is:
- /Free over Zero./ $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$ has no parameters and strictly beats Zero
  (modeling the increment $z_t-z_{t-1}$ beats modeling $z_t$ from scratch for any
  persistent latent), with the cleanest conditioning $J_t=(I-K_t)$
  ([[*The encoder (identity-framed blend)]]).
- /Free of bookkeeping./ It deletes the $\mu_p$ MLP and its stage-1 pretraining,
  the pinned/learnable/anchor knobs ($\lambda_{\mu_p}$, the anchor regularizer), the
  shared-vs-own-copy choice, and the state-conditional $\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ head
  with its log-variance regularizer ($\lambda_{\sigma_p}$) — each a hyperparameter
  and a failure surface. (It also makes model-v2's "$\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ discarded
  after handoff" /true again/: with $\sigma_0=1$ fixed there is no
  $\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ head in stage 2.)

So a learned $\mu_p$ trades a pile of error-prone machinery for a regime-dependent
efficiency gain. Start at identity; earn $\mu_p$.

** When to upgrade (and to what)

Add a baseline only when you /see the symptom/: $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ sitting at
signal scale, poor samples on clean-drift data, or weak-evidence/forecasting tasks
where the prediction must carry the step. Diagnose by the spectral norm $\|F_p\|$ of
the learned drift and the increment statistics $\mathbb E\|z_t-z_{t-1}\|$ vs
$\mathbb E\|z_t\|$ (small structured increment $\to$ identity; large structured
increment $\to$ baseline).

When you do, reach for *Linear* ($Az_{t-1}+b$) before *MLP*: it captures the cases
that actually need a baseline — rotation (oscillation), advection/propagation — with
a single matrix and none of the MLP's pretraining fragility. MLP only if Linear
demonstrably leaves structure on the table. (The value of an /expressive/ $\mu_p$
also /shrinks with multimodality/: a point can only capture the mean, which for a
multimodal conditional is a low-density valley.)

*Per-dataset prior* (predicted /Baseline-form/ ablation outcomes):
- *EEG* — oscillatory, a structured rotation far from identity $\Rightarrow$ Linear
  (MLP only if nonlinear). The increment is large and /predictable/, so a baseline
  absorbs it cheaply.
- *Video, Traffic, Wildfire* — persistence-dominated, the unpredictable part
  multimodal $\Rightarrow$ Identity (try Linear for traffic/wildfire propagation).
  The increment is small or unpredictable, so a point $\mu_p$ is a poor summary; let
  the diffusion $+$ evidence carry it.

It is the /latent/ transition (not the observable) and $\Delta t$ that decide:
coarse $\Delta t$ / compressed latents enlarge increments (toward a baseline), fine
$\Delta t$ shrinks them (toward identity). Measure, don't assume.

** Endpoints: A and C

- *C* (current) $=$ /Zero/ baseline with a free combiner: $J_t$ unpinned,
  $\|\prod_r J_r\|\sim\rho^{t-s}$ if $\rho>1$ — the original $\phi$-gradient blow-up.
- *A* $=$ /Identity/ baseline. Bare A ($\mu_t=z_{t-1}+g_\phi$, free $g_\phi$) tames
  $J_t\approx I$ but mis-centers under real drift; /our/ identity encoder is A in
  /blend/ form (z-free $m_t^b$ $+$ gate), which adds the $(I-K_t)$ damping and the
  $q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ init.
- The /Baseline-mode/ knob ([[file:model-v2.org::*Baseline-mode variants][Baseline-mode variants]]) — pinned vs. learnable-with-anchor,
  shared vs. own-copy — only bites once $\mu_p$ has parameters; for Identity it is
  degenerate (pinned). ($\psi$-gradients are benign throughout — the score net at
  step $t$ sees only the realized pair $(z_{t-1},z_t)$.)

* Why the gain form dominates

** Gradient conditioning (a construction fact, not a Gaussian claim)

Reparametrize ($\eta$ fixed); since $\mu_t$ reads the realized $z_{t-1}$,
\[
\frac{dz_t}{d\phi}=\sum_{s\le t}\Bigl(\prod_{r=s+1}^t J_r\Bigr)\frac{\partial z_s}{\partial\phi}\Big|_{\text{local}},
\qquad J_r=(I-K_r) + (\partial K_r/\partial z_{r-1})\odot(m_r^b-z_{r-1})
\]
(identity baseline, $F_p=I$). The dominant term is the gate contracting toward $0$,
so the through-time product stays controlled /whatever the SSM noise is/ (the
gain-sensitivity term is small — [[*Properties (all hold for arbitrary noise — they are construction facts)][Properties]]). This is the real fix for C's
blow-up; it does not rely on the conditional being Gaussian (only on how we wrote
$\mu_t$).

** Initialization and inductive bias

Gate-closed init $\Rightarrow q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ (KL $\approx0$),
recurrence at $J_t=I$ — the best-conditioned start. The inductive bias is "/persist
(stay at $z_{t-1}$), then correct by evidence/" — the random-walk frame made
well-conditioned by the gate, without C's unanchored freedom. Variance is free with
$\sigma_0=1$ ([[*Variance: free vs coupled]]); the gate drives only the mean, where the
conditioning benefit lives.

** Relation to the true (non-Gaussian) conditional

The encoder targets the /general/ conditional $\propto p(z_t\mid z_{t-1})\beta_t$,
of which the gain form is the LG-limit exact case and, away from it, a
well-conditioned Gaussian /inductive bias/. It inherits the right qualitative
structure (prediction $+$ gated evidence, evidence-vanishing at init) without
pretending the closed-form gain is the truth.

* Design knobs and open questions

** Blend vs scale (decided: blend)

The mean correction has two algebraically identical writings ($\delta_t=m_t^b-z_{t-1}$):
/scale/ $\mu_t=z_{t-1}+K_t\odot\delta_t$ and /blend/ $\mu_t=(1-K_t)z_{t-1}+K_t\odot m_t^b$.
Under the identity baseline ($F_p=I$) they diverge once the evidence read-out is
constrained:
- *Blend, $m_t^b$ z-free* (coordinate-queried, never $z$): the innovation's
  $-K_t z_{t-1}$ damps the recurrence,
  \(J_t=(I-K_t)+(\partial K_t/\partial z_{t-1})(m_t^b-z_{t-1})\) — a contraction
  /below/ $I$; where the future is informative ($K_t\to1$), $z_{t-1}$-sensitivity
  $\to0$ (the smoother property).
- *Scale, $\delta_t$ z-dependent*: \(J_t=I+\partial(K_t\odot\delta_t)/\partial z_{t-1}\)
  — pinned at $\approx I$, and the free correction can push it /above/ $I$; evidence
  cannot shut the recurrence off.
(At $K_t\to1$: $\|J_{\mathrm{blend}}\|\to0$ vs $\|J_{\mathrm{scale}}\|\gtrsim1$.)

*Decision: blend with z-free $m_t^b$.* It lets informative evidence contract the
recurrence below the identity ($J_t\to0$ as $K_t\to1$) — valuable whenever the
future disambiguates the state, and a safety margin even if the latent dynamics turn
out expansive; scale can only hold at $\approx I$ or worse. Only the evidence
/estimate/ $m_t^b$ is z-free; the gate $K_t$ still sees $z_{t-1}$ (via $U$). Cost: a
separate coordinate-queried evidence trunk ($E$ in [[*What to build]]). /When scale is
fine:/ if the evidence is weak everywhere ($K_t\approx0$, both $\approx I$), the
single state-conditioned feature suffices and is cheaper.

** Diagonal vs full gain

The gate is diagonal (a per-dim sigmoid, $K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$), so
$K_t\in(0,1)^D$ automatically. Three reasons not to go full:
1. /Closed-form pipeline./ A full gate (full $\Sigma=(I-K_t)Q$ under coupling)
   breaks the per-dim ESM/EDM closed forms ([[file:model-v2.org::*Closed-form target score][Closed-form target score]]): the target score
   would need a matrix inverse and the EDM constants are per-dim. The diagonal
   posterior is the load-bearing commitment; the diagonal gate is its consistent
   expression.
2. /Redundant for the mean./ $m_t^b$ already mixes dimensions (a free head on
   $h_t$), so a full gate buys no reachable-mean expressiveness — cross-dim
   structure lives in $m_t^b$, the gate only sets per-dim trust.
3. /Validity./ A diagonal sigmoid gate is automatically a contraction
   $0\prec K_t\prec I$; a naively-parameterized full gate (elementwise sigmoid on a
   matrix) need not be — e.g. $K=\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}0&.9\\.9&0\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$
   has $\|I-K\|=1.9>1$, so the damping is lost; enforcing $0\preceq K\preceq I$ for
   a full matrix needs an expensive eigen/Cholesky parameterization.
/Honest caveat:/ the exact LG gain $Q(Q+P^b)^{-1}$ is full (the backward message
couples dims through the dynamics), so diagonal $K_t$ /is/ an approximation — but
exactly the mean-field one already made by the diagonal posterior. /Open:/
low-rank-plus-diagonal $P_t^b$ (hence $K_t$) if cross-dim smoothing matters,
paired with free variance.

** Variance: free vs coupled

With the identity baseline we use /free/ variance
$\log\boldsymbol\sigma_t=W_\sigma U+b_\sigma$, $b_\sigma=0$ so $\sigma_0=1$ at init —
no Gaussian variance law imposed; the data sets the per-state / per-$t$ spread
through $W_\sigma U$. The /coupled/ law
$\boldsymbol\sigma_t^2=(1-K_t)\boldsymbol\sigma_p^2$ (precision addition) needs a
$\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ head, i.e. it is only available if you opt into a Linear/MLP
baseline ([[*The baseline knob: identity vs a learned baseline]]); even then it is an ablation, since
the gated /mean/ blend is robust off the LG limit but the variance /law/ $(1-K_t)$
is Gaussian-specific.

** Should the gain see the previous latent?

Yes — through $U$. The evidence /estimate/ $m_t^b$ is z-free (d-separation: $\beta_t$
does not depend on $z_{t-1}$), which is what gives the damping. The /gate/
$K_t=\mathrm{sigmoid}(W_K U+b_K)$ reads the state-conditioned feature $U$ (evidence
$+$ lifted $z_{t-1}$), so it /does/ see $z_{t-1}$ — it sets /how much/ evidence to
trust and /where to look/. (Under the identity baseline with $\sigma_0=1$ the
general gain form's prior-log-variance feature $\alpha\odot\ell_p$ is $0$ and drops
out; the gate's $z$-dependence is entirely through $U$.) This enters only the
controlled $\partial K_t$ Jacobian term, never the dominant $(I-K_t)$
([[*Properties (all hold for arbitrary noise — they are construction facts)][Properties]]).

* What to build

A concrete grid-free realization (transformer evidence-gathering, per-site
read-outs): the *identity-framed blend* with a *z-free evidence estimate* and fixed
$\sigma_0=1$. Latents live on $N$ sites with coordinates $P$; observations are
summarized by $h_t$ at coordinates $Q$. /No prior heads/ — $\mu_p=z_{t-1}$,
$\sigma_0=1$.

#+begin_src text
# 1. z-FREE evidence trunk E -> m^b   (NO state in the query => ∂m^b/∂z = 0 => damping)
E = γ(P)                                      # [N,C]  query = coordinates only
for l = 1..L_e:
    E += MHCrossAttn(query=E, key/value = h_t + γ(Q))   # z-free evidence gather [N,C]
    E += Mix(E)                                         # spatial mixing
    # pre-LayerNorm each sub-block
m_b = W_m E + b_m                            # evidence mean m^b   (z-FREE)

# 2. STATE-CONDITIONED head -> gate, std   (query sees z; full attention power)
U = E + MLP_lift(z_{t-1})                     # seed query with state
for l = 1..L_u:
    U += MHCrossAttn(query=U, key/value = h_t + γ(Q))   # state-conditioned evidence gather
    U += Mix(U)
K_t = sigmoid(W_K U + b_K)                    # gate ∈ (0,1)^D; b_K ≪ 0 init (gate closed)
s_t = W_σ U + b_σ                            # log-std; b_σ = 0 init  (σ_0 = 1)

# 3. IDENTITY-framed blend, then sample
μ_t   = z_{t-1} + K_t ⊙ (m_b − z_{t-1})       # = (1−K_t)⊙z_{t-1} + K_t⊙m_b;  ≈ z_{t-1} at init
σ_t   = exp(s_t)                              # = 1 at init
z_t   = μ_t + σ_t ⊙ η_t
#+end_src

Key points:
- /Identity frame, $\sigma_0=1$./ $\mu_t$ centers on $z_{t-1}$; $\sigma_t$ starts at
  $1$. Gate closed at init ($b_K\ll0$) $\Rightarrow q\approx\mathcal N(z_{t-1},I)$ —
  the random-walk prior — so KL$\,\approx0$ at handoff. No $\mu_p$ /
  $\boldsymbol\sigma_p$ heads, and no prior-variance gate feature (it is $0$).
- /z-free $m_t^b$ $\Rightarrow$ damping./ $m_t^b$ reads the coordinate-queried trunk
  $E$ (never $z$), so $\partial m_t^b/\partial z=0$ and
  $J_t=(I-K_t)+(\partial K_t/\partial z_{t-1})(m_t^b-z_{t-1})$ — a contraction below
  $I$, $\to0$ where the future is informative ([[*Blend vs scale (decided: blend)]]).
- /Gate/std keep full state-conditioned attention./ $K_t,s_t$ read $U$ — a full
  $L_u$-block $\mathrm{MHCrossAttn}(\mathrm{query}{=}U)$ stack built on $E$ — so the
  state controls /how much/ to trust evidence /and where to look/; only $m_t^b$'s
  trunk is z-free. The two query streams share the keys/values $h_t+\gamma(Q)$.
- /Free variance./ $s_t=W_\sigma U+b_\sigma$ — unconstrained, $\sigma_0=1$ at init
  ([[*Variance: free vs coupled]]).
- /Downstream unchanged./ ELBO, the closed-form ESM target $s_q$, and EDM
  preconditioning all consume $(\mu_t,\boldsymbol\sigma_t)$ as before.
  $\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}$ (the increment spread, for EDM) is tracked/fixed
  separately and is /not/ $\sigma_0$.
- /Opt-in baseline./ To add a Linear/MLP $\mu_p$ (e.g. EEG), replace the identity
  frame $z_{t-1}$ with $\mu_p(z_{t-1})$ and reinstate the prior-log-variance gate
  feature $\alpha\odot\ell_p$; see [[*The baseline knob: identity vs a learned baseline]].

** Mix: default site self-attention

$\mathrm{Mix}:(\mathbb R^{N\times C},P)\to\mathbb R^{N\times C}$ couples the $N$ latent
sites among themselves — the cross-attention only links sites to /observations/,
whereas $\mathrm{Mix}$ links sites to /each other/ (it is the latent-field operator).
The default is a pre-LN transformer block over sites, made coordinate-aware through
$\gamma(P)$:
\[
\mathrm{Mix}(U) = U + \mathrm{MHSelfAttn}\!\bigl(q{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)+\gamma(P),\ k{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)+\gamma(P),\ v{=}\mathrm{LN}(U)\bigr),
\]
followed by a feed-forward residual $U\leftarrow U+\mathrm{FFN}(\mathrm{LN}(U))$. It is
permutation-equivariant, coordinate-aware (positions enter only via $\gamma(P)$), and
handles variable $N$ — the /same/ block works on any point set (grid, mesh, graph
nodes, channels). Cost $O(N^2C)$ per block; for large $N$ swap a sub-quadratic
$\mathrm{Mix}$ (windowed/axial attention, a Perceiver $N\!\to\!M\!\to\!N$ bottleneck,
or k-NN graph attention) behind the same interface. In the z-free trunk $E$,
$\mathrm{Mix}$ reads only $(E,P)$ — no state — so $m_t^b$ stays z-free.
