This document specifies the experimental design for evaluating the
baseline-centering and pretraining scheme defined in =model-v2.org=. The
math, notation, and protocol live in =model-v2.org=; this file specifies
*what we measure*, *what we sweep*, and *how we report*. It does not
restate equations — see the pointer table in § Pointers to =model-v2.org=
below.

* Implementation precursors

The following items must be implemented before the init experiments
can run. They are /not/ ablation knobs; they are baked-in design
choices established in =model-v2.org= that the codebase needs to
support.
These are stated with \(j=1\), but should be implemented for any \(j\).

- *VHP for \(p(z_1)\)* (=model-v2.org= § Reframing VHP to use diffusion).
  Requires:
  (i) auxiliary latent \(z_0 \in \mathbb{R}^D\) with prior
  \(p(z_0) = \mathcal{N}(0, I)\);
  (ii) variational posterior \(q_Φ(z_0 \mid z_1)\) — a small
  diagonal-Gaussian MLP head;
  (iii) reuse of the shared transition heads at \(t = 1\)
  (\(p_ψ(z_1 \mid z_0)\) is the Gaussian transition in stage 1 and the
  reverse-SDE density driven by the same \(s_ψ\) in stage 2);
  (iv) extension of the \(σ_{\mathrm{data}}^2[t]\) buffer to
  \(t \in \{1, \ldots, T\}\) so the stage-2 ESM surrogate at \(t = 1\)
  gets EDM preconditioning.

- *Zero-initialise the final layer of \(F_ψ\)*
  (=model-v2.org= § EDM Preconditioning). At handoff, the EDM
  denoiser-network \(F_ψ\) is created with its last layer zero-initialised
  so that \(D_ψ \approx c_{\mathrm{skip}}\, \tilde{z}_t^{(τ)}\) at stage-2
  start. Standard "zero-init residual" pattern; not an ablation knob.

- *Padding mask in the diffusion side-info tensor*
  (=model-v2.org= § Reframing VHP to use diffusion, /Padding mask in
  the diffusion side-info tensor/). The score net's side-info tensor
  carries a binary mask indicating whether the previous-latent
  conditioning slot holds a real latent (\(z_{t-1}\) for \(t \geq 2\)) or
  the padded auxiliary (\(z_0\) at \(t = 1\) under the VHP). This lets a
  single \(s_ψ\) handle both cases without duplicating the network.

* Goal

Empirically evaluate the 3-axis ablation grid defined in =model-v2.org=
§ Ablation grid summary (baseline form × baseline mode × tracking mode),
crossed with two new continuous knobs introduced by the handoff protocol
of =model-v2.org= § Stage-1 → stage-2 handoff:

- \(N_{\mathrm{pretrain}}\): number of stage-1 pretraining steps (the
  /amount of pretraining/).
- \(σ_{\mathrm{pert}}\): magnitude of the Gaussian parameter noise added
  to all encoder weights at the handoff.

For each /cell/ (a categorical configuration of the 3-axis grid), we run
a hyperparameter study with Optuna over the two new knobs and the
remaining tuned hyperparams of the training pipeline. We then compare
cells by the best-trial value of a single headline objective, logging
the full metric set below for post-hoc analysis.

* Headline metrics (top 6, for tables and plots)

These six metrics are the deliverable axis of comparison across cells.
Each is logged once per trial (or per-step for time-series metrics).

1. *PF-ODE NLL* on held-out observations (cf. =todos.org= line 81: "log
   likelihood, via probability-flow ODE"). The principled likelihood
   number; gated on PF-ODE-likelihood implementation. Falls back to
   metric (2) until that's available.

2. *Stage-2 ELBO surrogate* — cross-entropy term via Monte Carlo plus
   analytic entropy of \(q_φ\). Definition: see =model-v2.org= § ELBO
   contribution and § Regression target and final loss.

3. *Transition JSD on ground-truth latents* (simulated data only; cf.
   =todos.org= line 79: "jsd for the relevant exps"). The only metric
   that isolates the /transition/ model from the encoder: compare samples
   from \(p_ψ(z_t \mid z_{t-1})\) against samples from the known
   ground-truth transition. Reported per \(t\) and aggregated.

4. *CRPS-sum* across forecast horizons, in both latent and observation
   spaces (cf. =todos.org= line 78). Sums the CRPS across dimensions for
   a proper-scoring-rule summary of probabilistic forecast quality.

5. *Wall-clock time to a target metric.* Records the wall-clock time
   (and parallel curve in diffusion-model NFEs) to reach a fixed
   threshold of metric (1) or (2). This is the x-axis of the
   perf-to-wallclock plot called for in =todos.org= line 69-72.

6. *\(σ_{\mathrm{data}}^2(t)\) drift trajectory* + two-component
   decomposition. Tracks the EMA buffer over the course of stage 2,
   alongside its two components (average posterior variance
   \(\mathbb{E}[\|\boldsymbol{σ}_t\|^2]\) and spread of residual means
   \(\mathrm{tr}(\mathrm{Var}[\hat{μ}_t])\)). Definition and
   decomposition: =model-v2.org= § Data-variance tracking. The
   headline diagnostic for the baseline-mode × tracking-mode
   interaction predicted by =model-v2.org= § Interaction with
   tracking-mode variants.

* Secondary metrics (logged, not headlined)

Logged every trial for post-hoc analysis but not used as a comparison
axis in the main tables/figures.

** Forecasting

- MAE / MSE at horizons \(h \in \{1, 5, 10, T-1\}\).
- Sample-mean CRPS per horizon (decomposes metric (4) above).

** Training dynamics

- Stage-2 diffusion loss aggregated and binned by \(τ\) (10 quantile
  bins) along with batch variance per bin.
- Encoder mean drift \(\|\mathbb{E}[μ_t] - \mathbb{E}[μ_t^{(0)}]\|\) per
  \(t\).
- Encoder posterior variance \(\mathbb{E}[\|\boldsymbol{σ}_t\|^2]\) per
  \(t\).
- Reconstruction-loss term of the ELBO.
- \(μ_p\) drift \(\|μ_p - μ_p^{(0)}\|\) on a fixed validation set of
  \(z_{t-1}\) samples (Learnable mode only).
- Structural-degeneracy-direction projection of the \(μ_p\) change
  (Learnable mode only). Defined in =model-v2.org= § Baseline-mode
  variants (structural-degeneracy argument).
- Stage-2 ESM surrogate evaluated at \(t = 1\) (the initial-state term
  \(\mathcal{L}_1^{(\mathrm{init})}\) under the VHP), tracked
  alongside the per-\(t\) ESM losses at \(t \geq 2\). Looking for
  parity in scale — large deviations indicate the VHP initial-state
  conditional is not training comparably to the interior transitions.
- \(\mathrm{KL}\bigl[q_Φ(z_0 \mid z_1) \,\|\, \mathcal{N}(0, I)\bigr]\)
  averaged over the batch, per training step. Should rise off zero
  early in stage 1 and stabilise; collapse back to zero indicates
  \(q_Φ\) is ignoring \(z_1\) (VHP degenerated to the homoskedastic
  prior).

** Compute and efficiency

- Wall-clock per epoch.
- Gradient steps to convergence.
- Diffusion-sampling NFEs vs. quality (post-training).
- Peak GPU memory.
- Parameter count per submodule (encoder, decoder, baseline,
  auxiliary posterior \(q_Φ\), diffusion net separately).
- Throughput (samples/sec) at sampling time, parameterized by NFEs.

** Sampling quality (post-training)

- Sampling-NFE vs. metric trade-off curves for metrics (1), (2), (4).

** Stability and identifiability

- \(\|\nabla_ψ\|\), \(\|\nabla_φ\|\) per step.
- NaN/Inf rate per loss component.
- Catastrophic-collapse counter: triggered when
  \(σ_{\mathrm{data}}^2[t]\) exits a range or when the ELBO surrogate
  regresses below the stage-1 close.
- KL gap: stage-2 ELBO surrogate minus stage-1 close-of-training value
  (post-handoff progress).
- Residual-centering check \(\|\mathbb{E}[\hat{z}_t]\|\) for the
  parameter-free baseline forms (zero, identity).
- *\(\log \boldsymbol{σ}_p^2\) collapse-vs-inflation diagnostic.* At
  the end of stage 1, log the per-\((t, d)\) batch mean of
  \(\log \boldsymbol{σ}_p^2(z_{t-1})_d\) across an evaluation
  trajectory sample. Flag any \((t, d)\) whose mean deviates more
  than a few standard deviations from zero — the global anchor
  \(\mathcal{R}_{σ_p}\) can be satisfied by some dims collapsing
  while others inflate (balancing in aggregate), and this diagnostic
  surfaces that pathology. If triggered, the remediation is to
  tighten \(λ_{σ_p}\) or fall back to a per-\((t, d)\) anchor (see
  =model-v2.org= § State-conditional prior variance).
- *\(q_Φ\) posterior summary.* Mean and variance of the sampled
  \(z_0 \sim q_Φ(z_0 \mid z_1)\) across a validation batch. Sanity
  check that the auxiliary posterior is informative (its variance is
  meaningfully less than \(\mathcal{N}(0, I)\) and its mean varies
  with \(z_1\)).

* Hyperparameters introduced by this experiment

Two new continuous knobs are exposed by the handoff protocol and were
not present in the prior methodology. They are tuned per cell by Optuna.
All /other/ hyperparams in the training pipeline (learning rates per
parameter group, EMA decay \(γ\), \(μ_p\)-anchor strength \(λ_{μ_p}\),
\(\boldsymbol{σ}_p\)-log-variance regularizer strength \(λ_{σ_p}\)
(starting suggestion \(10^{-2}\) per =model-v2.org= § State-conditional
prior variance), encoder posterior-σ floor, etc.) are tuned by the
implementation; this section specifies *only* the ranges for the two
new knobs because they are specific to this experiment.

| New knob                  | Suggested Optuna range                                          | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                |
|---------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------------+------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| \(N_{\mathrm{pretrain}}\) | log-uniform over \(\approx \{50, \ldots, 2000\}\) stage-1 steps; include \(0\) as a control | On small synthetic data \(\approx 1000\) stage-2 steps already converges without any init, so stage-1 budgets in the same order of magnitude are sufficient. Pair with a stage-1 ELBO-plateau early-stop so trials don't waste compute. |
| \(σ_{\mathrm{pert}}\)     | log-uniform over \(\approx \{10^{-4}, \ldots, 10^{-1}\}\); include \(0\) as a control       | Encoder weights typically scale \(\sim 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{fan\_in}} \approx 10^{-2}\); a one-decade window around that brackets "imperceptible" to "destructive".                                       |

Including \(0\) in support for both knobs gives us the "no pretraining"
and "no perturbation" controls without requiring a separate trial config.

* Fixed handoff-protocol decisions (not tuned)

These are choices in the handoff protocol that are /not/ exposed to
Optuna. Stated here so the implementation prompt does not introduce
them as knobs.

- *Noise-injection target*: full encoder weights only. No
  variance-head-only or last-layer-only variants.
- *Optimizer rebuild scope*: full reset, always. No partial-reset or
  carry-over variants.
- *\(σ_{\mathrm{data}}^2[t]\) buffer continuity at handoff*: the
  buffer is unified across stage 1 and stage 2 — it accumulates
  passively throughout stage 1, carries over to stage 2 without
  re-initialisation, and only the EMA /schedule/ (step counter, bias
  correction, decay annealing) resets at the handoff. Under /Fixed/
  tracking the buffer is then frozen at its handoff value; under
  /Global EMA/ and /Per-\(t\) EMA/ the updates resume from that value
  with the reset schedule (see =model-v2.org= § Stage-1 → stage-2
  handoff steps 1 and 5).
- *Zero-initialise \(F_ψ\)'s final layer* at the start of stage 2
  (see =model-v2.org= § EDM Preconditioning).
- *Padding mask in the diffusion side-info tensor* (see Implementation
  precursors and =model-v2.org= § Reframing VHP to use diffusion).

* Composition with the ablation grid

- *Categorical cells.* The 18-cell grid of =model-v2.org= § Ablation
  grid summary, derived from the three knobs (Baseline form ×
  Baseline mode × Tracking mode) with the parameter-free baseline
  forms (Zero, Identity) admitting only the Pinned baseline-mode:

  | Baseline forms      | × | Baseline modes        | × | Tracking modes | = | Cells |
  |---------------------+---+-----------------------+---+----------------+---+-------|
  | zero, identity (2)  | × | Pinned only           | × | 3              | = | 6     |
  | linear, MLP    (2)  | × | Pinned, Learnable (2) | × | 3              | = | 12    |

  Net: *18 cells*. These are /not/ Optuna search dimensions; they are
  the experimental factor of interest.

- *Per cell.* One Optuna study, single-objective on metric (1) when
  available else (2). TPE sampler, median pruner on the objective. Trial
  count budgeted by the implementation. All metrics in §§ Headline
  metrics and Secondary metrics are logged per trial for post-hoc
  analysis but do not influence the sampler or pruner.

- *Reporting.* For each cell, report (a) the best trial's metric set,
  (b) the distribution of trial outcomes (median, IQR over trials),
  (c) the headline plot of metric (1)/(2) versus metric (5) wall clock.
  Headline tables index cells against the 6 headline metrics.

- *Pilot.* Before launching the full grid, run a small Optuna study (a
  handful of trials) on a single canonical cell — e.g., MLP baseline /
  Pinned / per-\(t\) EMA — to validate the metric pipeline, Optuna
  integration, and logging cadence.

* Pairwise-comparison stories

These are the questions the cell-vs-cell comparisons are designed to
answer. They mirror =model-v2.org= § Pairwise comparisons (under
§ Baseline-form variants) and § Tracking-mode variants:

- *Baseline form*: does residual decomposition help? Does learning the
  baseline beyond identity help? Does nonlinear capacity in the baseline
  help?
- *Baseline mode (Pinned vs. Learnable)*: does the soft anchor
  outperform hard pinning? How much does \(μ_p\) drift under Learnable
  (logged), and is that drift along the structural-degeneracy direction
  or genuinely useful?
- *Tracking mode (Fixed / global EMA / per-\(t\) EMA)*: does the
  implicit-unit-variance regularizer of Fixed help or hurt? Does
  \(t\)-resolved tracking pay off?
- *Pretraining amount* \(N_{\mathrm{pretrain}}\): does the centering
  scheme need a "soak" pretraining stage at all, or is the same
  end-state reached by stage 2 alone?
- *Encoder perturbation* \(σ_{\mathrm{pert}}\): how much encoder noise
  is needed to escape stage-1 geometry without destroying the stage-1
  alignment with \(μ_p^{(0)}\)?
- *Interaction* (=model-v2.org= § Interaction with tracking-mode
  variants): under Learnable \(μ_p\), does the gap
  between tracking-mode variants (2) and (3) shrink? Headline metric
  (6) is the direct test.
